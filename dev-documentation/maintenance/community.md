# Community & Standardization

The ontology maintenance strategy is guided not only by methodological and technical considerations but also by community engagement and collaborative standardization practices.

## W3C Community Group for CE and DPPs

Onto-DESIDE established a **W3C Community Group** focused on Circular Economy and Digital Product Passports (DPPs), with the group identifier `ce-dpp`.

W3C Community Groups are informal open forums within the World Wide Web Consortium where participants share and discuss ideas related to web technologies. They serve as a first step towards formal technical web standards.

### Mission

The group's mission is to support the technical implementation of infrastructure and applications for the Circular Economy and Digital Product Passports. Key activities include:

- Discussing requirements set by CE and DPPs on web technologies and standards
- Identifying standardization challenges not yet addressed
- Sharing experiences and ideas on usage of web technologies in CE
- Complementing the work of ISO/TC 323 on CE standardization from a technical web perspective

### Ongoing discussion topics

- Practical applications of the developed ontologies and their standardization needs
- Feedback on challenges in implementation and use of existing web standards in CE and DPP
- Data models specific for CE and DPP
- Best practices in data sharing
- Ontology integration across platforms and domains

!!! tip "Join the group"
    The W3C Community Group is open to all parties interested in the technical aspects of CE and DPP implementation. [Join here](https://www.w3.org/community/ce-dpp/)

## RDA FAIR Mappings Working Group

To ensure that ontology alignments are as interoperable and reusable as possible, Onto-DESIDE joined the **FAIR Mappings Working Group** at the Research Data Alliance (RDA).

### Objectives

The working group collaborates with various communities to develop common guidelines making ontology mappings **FAIR** — Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reusable.

### CEON contribution

CEON-related mappings were proposed as a case study within the working group, providing example mappings to support the group's goals. This complements alignment work at other venues such as the [CE track at OAEI](methodology.md#ce-track-at-oaei).

More information:
